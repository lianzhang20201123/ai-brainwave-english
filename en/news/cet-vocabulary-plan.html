A Smarter Vocabulary Plan for CET-4 and CET-6

A phased approach for university students and adult learners preparing for CET exams.

What makes CET vocabulary difficult

For many learners, the issue is not a lack of words, but unstable retention and slow retrieval during reading and listening. A better plan focuses on coverage, review, and exam use—not only on word list accumulation.

Phase 1: expand coverage

In the early stage, build broad familiarity with high-frequency exam vocabulary. The goal is to map the territory and reduce blank areas rather than perfect every word immediately.

Phase 2: reinforce and revisit

In the middle phase, repeated exposure matters more. Core words should keep reappearing through structured review and reassessment so that familiarity becomes stable.

Phase 3: connect vocabulary to tasks

As the exam approaches, vocabulary work should serve reading, listening, translation, and writing. Recognition is not enough; learners need fast access under time pressure.

Advice for busy adult learners

A steady weekly rhythm is usually more effective than occasional all-day cramming. Sustainable pacing often wins over intensity.
