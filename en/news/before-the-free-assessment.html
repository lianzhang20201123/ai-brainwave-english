How to Prepare for the Free Assessment

A simple guide for parents, students, and adult learners before their first on-site assessment.

Know what the assessment is for

The purpose of the free assessment is not to judge whether a learner is “good enough.” It is meant to clarify the starting point: phonics readiness, approximate vocabulary level, and the most suitable pace for future training.

A little preparation helps

Before the visit, it helps to know the student’s grade, recent English exam performance, whether phonics was learned before, and what the current pain point is. Adult learners can also share their exam target or study purpose.

Honest feedback matters more than performance anxiety

There is no need to perform for the assessment. The clearer the starting condition, the more useful the follow-up recommendation becomes.

Focus on three outcomes

After the assessment, the most helpful takeaways are usually the starting vocabulary level, whether phonics needs reinforcement, and what training pace is recommended.

Assess first, decide later

In many cases, taking the assessment first saves time because course selection becomes clearer once the starting point is visible.

Next step

Clarify the starting point before choosing the next training path

A free on-site assessment usually makes program choice, pacing, and expectations much easier to set.
